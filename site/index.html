Service businesses live and die by reply speed.

A homeowner messages five aircon companies on a Saturday afternoon. Whoever replies first usually wins the job. Whoever replies on Monday is talking to someone else's customer.

But the same person quoting jobs is also the one fixing them. You can't sit on chat all day. Hiring a replier is slow, expensive, and never quite sounds like you. Pointing a generic AI chatbot at customers is worse — it makes things up, alienates people, and has no idea what your prices are.

Chiefpa does the boring middle thing: it drafts, you approve, and it learns. Until one day the questions you've been answering identically for months just… stop landing in your inbox. They've already been replied to. In your voice. Logged in your CRM. Booked on your calendar.

How it works

1

A customer messages.

On WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, or Telegram. Chiefpa reads it, looks up who they are in your wiki, checks their history and your prices, and drafts a reply.

2

You approve, edit, or reject.

Every draft lands in your inbox. One tap to send. If you edit, Chiefpa watches the edit. If you reject, it tries again. Nothing goes to a customer without you.

3

Patterns earn auto-pilot.

When you've approved the same kind of question the same way at least five times across five different days with zero edits, that pattern is eligible for auto-send. You decide if you want to flip the switch.

What's built in

No bolt-ons, no setup safari. The four channels, the CRM, the calendar, and the learning loop ship together.

Four channels, one inbox

WhatsApp (official + sidecar), Messenger, Instagram DMs, and Telegram all land in the same inbox. Same approve/edit/send flow regardless of where the customer wrote from.

A wiki that writes itself

After every conversation, Chiefpa updates the customer's record. New address, new aircon model, rescheduled appointment — it's already noted by the time you check.

Calendar baked in

"Confirmed for Tuesday 10am" in a chat becomes a calendar event. "Move to Thursday" becomes an update. The conversation and the schedule never drift apart.

Learns your voice, not a template

Chiefpa watches what you approve and what you edit. The drafts get more like you over time. There's no prompt to write, no persona to fill in, no template library to maintain.

Owner-authoritative auto-pilot

A pattern only goes auto-send when you flip the switch — never automatically. Promote it for FAQ-style questions, keep human-in-the-loop for everything that needs judgement.

Messaging-window aware

Meta's 24-hour reply window is enforced for you. Outside it, Chiefpa offers an escape hatch instead of pretending the message went through.
